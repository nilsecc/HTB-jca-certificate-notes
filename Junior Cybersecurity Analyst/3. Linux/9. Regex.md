### Basic Usage

**grep (basic)**  
`grep "text" file`  
Search for a literal string

**grep extended regex**  
`grep -E "pattern" file`  
Enable advanced regex (recommended)

---

### Anchors

**Start of line**  
`grep -E "^root" /etc/passwd`  
Match lines starting with "root"

**End of line**  
`grep -E "bash$" /etc/passwd`  
Match lines ending with "bash"

---

### Wildcards

**. (any character)**  
`grep -E "r..t" file`  
Matches: root, raat, r1_t…

__._ (anything)_*  
`grep -E "root.*bash" /etc/passwd`  
Matches lines containing both "root" and "bash"

---

### Character Classes

**[abc]**  
`grep -E "[abc]" file`  
Match a OR b OR c

**[a-z]**  
`grep -E "[a-z]" file`  
Lowercase letters

**[^abc]**  
`grep -E "[^#]" file`  
Match anything EXCEPT #

---

### Quantifiers

*****  
`grep -E "ro*t" file`  
r + 0 or more o + t

**+**  
`grep -E "ro+t" file`  
r + 1 or more o + t

**{n}**  
`grep -E "o{2}" file`  
Exactly 2 o

**{n,m}**  
`grep -E "o{1,3}" file`  
Between 1 and 3 o

---

### Grouping

**()**  
`grep -E "(root|admin)" file`  
Group expressions

---

### OR Operator

**|**  
`grep -E "(root|admin)" /etc/passwd`  
Match root OR admin

---

### AND (order matters)

**.***  
`grep -E "root.*bash" /etc/passwd`  
Match "root" AND later "bash"

---

### Word Boundaries

**\b**  
`grep -E "\bPermit" sshd_config`  
Word starting with Permit

`grep -E "Authentication\b" sshd_config`  
Word ending with Authentication

---

### Negation

**-v**  
`grep -v "#" file`  
Exclude lines with #

---

### Practical Examples

**Lines NOT containing #**  
`grep -Ev "^#|^$" sshd_config`

**Lines with words starting with Permit**  
`grep -E "\bPermit" sshd_config`

**Lines with words ending in Authentication**  
`grep -E "Authentication\b" sshd_config`

**Lines containing Key**  
`grep "Key" sshd_config`

**Lines starting with Password and containing yes**  
`grep -E "^Password.*yes" sshd_config`

**Lines ending with yes**  
`grep -E "yes$" sshd_config`

---

### Useful Flags

**Ignore case**  
`grep -i "root" file`

**Show line numbers**  
`grep -n "root" file`

**Recursive search**  
`grep -r "root" .`

---

### Mental Model

- `^` → start
- `$` → end
- `.*` → anything
- `[]` → options
- `()` → groups
- `|` → OR
- `-v` → NOT
